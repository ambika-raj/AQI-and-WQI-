GENERALLY ABOUT AIR AND WATER POLLUTION
Since every pollutant has a unique health risk profile, it is challenging to condense all pertinent information into a brief chapter.
Nonetheless, decision-makers and public health professionals in developing nations must be aware of the possible health hazards posed by water and air pollution as well as know where to look for the more precise knowledge needed to address a given circumstance.
This chapter will concentrate on the issues brought on by air and water pollution at the local, national, and international levels rather than rehashing the discussion of indoor air pollution from burning biomass and water pollution from inadequate sanitation at the household level.
